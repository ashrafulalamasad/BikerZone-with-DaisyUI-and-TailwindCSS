Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

You should replace your helmet if it is more than 5 years old, has been in a crash, or shows visible damage like cracks or dents. Even if it looks fine, the inner foam and materials weaken over time, reducing protection. It’s better to be safe and get a new one if you are unsure.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

Before riding a bike that has been in storage, it's important to check a few key components to ensure it's safe. Inspect the tires for proper inflation and any signs of damage, check the brakes for responsiveness, and ensure the chain is lubricated and functioning smoothly. If you're unsure, it's always a good idea to take it to a professional for a tune-up.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

Always wear a helmet, use hand signals when turning, and be aware of your surroundings. Follow traffic laws and ride in the same direction as traffic. Use bike lanes when available and be cautious at intersections.
